# Mission: Expositional Study of Isaiah (All 66 Chapters)

## Why
To know God more deeply and personally through a complete, careful, verse-by-verse pass through Isaiah, learning sound inductive Bible study method along the way. The goal is a changed walk with God, not just information: seeing the Holy One of Israel clearly and seeing Christ throughout the book.

## Success looks like
- Can read any passage in Isaiah and identify its context, structure, and main point without help
- Can practice the inductive method (observation, interpretation, application) unaided on a new passage
- Can explain Isaiah's storyline, major sections, and key themes (holiness, remnant, Servant, Zion, new creation) from memory
- Has memorized ~15 key verses, one per major section
- Can articulate how each major section points to Christ
- Regularly discusses what is being learned with people at church

## Constraints
- 20-30 minutes per day, daily cadence
- ESV is the study translation; owned tools are the MacArthur Study Bible and Olive Tree Bible Software
- New to serious/structured study: lessons must teach method foundations, not assume them
- Reformed framework; postmillennial (Durbin-style) home position on kingdom passages, with MacArthur (premil) and White (amil) divergences flagged honestly at the big passages
- Workflow per session: read the passage in ESV first and jot observations, then open the lesson

## Out of scope
- Hebrew language study (English-tools word study only)
- Academic critical scholarship beyond briefly answering the authorship/unity question
- Formal apologetics training (touch on Isa 53 objections when the text raises them, no more)
