Lesson 0008 · Unit 1: Chapters 1-6

Isaiah 4:2-6: The Branch and the Cleansed City

~25 min · Passage: Isaiah 4:2-6 (ESV, read first!) · First messianic "Branch" text

Did you read it?
If you have not yet read Isaiah 4:2-6 twice and written five observations, stop here and do it now. Five verses only, but every line is loaded; slow reading pays double in short passages.

Recall first

Observation check

Compare your list. Strong observations on this passage include:

  • "In that day" (v. 2): the same phrase that introduced judgment on the proud women of Zion (3:18) now introduces glory. Same day, two faces: judgment and salvation arrive together.
  • Two parallel growths (v. 2): "the branch of the LORD" is "beautiful and glorious", and "the fruit of the land" is "the pride and honor of the survivors". Something springs up from God's side and from the land's side.
  • The survivors again (vv. 2-3): "he who is left... who remains". The remnant thread from 1:9 continues, and now they are called "holy".
  • A register (v. 3): everyone "recorded for life in Jerusalem". A written list decides who belongs.
  • Washing language (v. 4): "washed away the filth of the daughters of Zion". This reaches straight back to 3:16-4:1; the very people just judged are the ones cleansed.
  • The means of cleansing (v. 4): "by a spirit of judgment and by a spirit of burning". Purity comes through fire, not around it.
  • Exodus scenery (v. 5): cloud by day, smoke and shining flame by night, over Mount Zion "and over her assemblies". Compare Exodus 13:21-22.
  • Shelter piled on shelter (vv. 5-6): canopy, booth, shade, refuge, shelter from storm and rain. The section that began with a courtroom (1:2) pauses under a roof God builds.

Interpretation

Who is "the branch of the LORD"?

In that day the branch of the LORD shall be beautiful and glorious, and the fruit of the land shall be the pride and honor of the survivors of Israel. Isaiah 4:2, ESV

"Branch" (Hebrew tsemach, a sprout or growth) becomes a fixed messianic title in the prophets: "I will raise up for David a righteous Branch" (Jer 23:5), "I will bring my servant the Branch" (Zech 3:8), and Isaiah's own "shoot from the stump of Jesse" (11:1). This verse plants the seed of that whole line. The Branch is "of the LORD": God's own growth, springing from God's initiative after the forest of human pride has been felled (2:12-17). Read canonically, this is the Messiah: modest as a sprout, yet "beautiful and glorious". Some interpreters take v. 2 as merely agricultural abundance; the parallel prophets and the pairing with "glorious" weigh heavily against that. Note the two sides of v. 2: the Branch of the LORD (the divine side) and the fruit of the land (the earthly side); in Jesus, God's Branch and David's fruit are one person.

"Recorded for life": the register of grace

Who survives the burning? Not the strongest or cleverest, but "everyone who has been recorded for life" (v. 3). A citizen register was a real civic object (Neh 7:5), but Scripture keeps using the image for God's own book: "everyone whose name shall be found written in the book" (Dan 12:1), "rejoice that your names are written in heaven" (Luke 10:20), the Lamb's book of life (Rev 21:27). Holiness in v. 3 is not achieved and then recorded; it flows from being recorded. This is Reformed theology's heartbeat in miniature: God's electing grace precedes and produces the holiness of his people.

Cleansing by judgment, presence for all

Verse 4 says Zion's filth is washed "by a spirit of judgment and by a spirit of burning". God does not lower the standard to keep the people; he purifies the people through the judgment itself. The fire that fell in chapter 3 is the same fire that leaves a holy remnant in chapter 4. Then v. 5 does something astonishing with the exodus: the pillar of cloud and fire that once hovered over one tent (Exod 40:34-38) now covers "the whole site of Mount Zion and... her assemblies". God's manifest presence, formerly localized at the tabernacle, spreads over every gathering of his people. The New Testament announces the fulfillment: the church is God's temple, his Spirit dwelling in the whole assembly (1 Cor 3:16, Eph 2:21-22).

Where your teachers stand
All three see the Messiah in the Branch; the fork is timing and locale of the full picture. MacArthur (premil): vv. 5-6 describe millennial Jerusalem after Christ's return. White (amil): fulfilled in the church now, consummated in the eternal state. Durbin (postmil, our home lens): the cleansed, sheltered, Spirit-covered Zion is the church in this age, growing toward the nations-streaming vision of 2:2-4 before Christ returns. The exegetical anchor shared by all: the Branch has come, and the register is open now.

Application

  • About God: he never judges his people to destroy them but to purify them. If you are under his discipline, the goal is v. 3: "called holy" (Heb 12:10-11).
  • About you: assurance does not rest on your performance but on being "recorded for life". Jesus said to rejoice precisely there (Luke 10:20). Is that where your confidence sits today?
  • About the church: the canopy is over "her assemblies". The sheltered place in a scorched world is the gathered people of God; do not drift from it.
