Memory Verses

One anchor verse per stretch of the book, ESV, full text below. A verse goes "active" when its unit begins; lessons keep reviewing earlier ones on a spaced schedule.

1. Isaiah 1:18 · Unit 1 (ch. 1-6)

"Come now, let us reason together, says the LORD: though your sins are like scarlet, they shall be as white as snow; though they are red like crimson, they shall become like wool." Isaiah 1:18, ESV

Why this one: the gospel in the lawsuit; scarlet sins made white as snow.

2. Isaiah 6:3 · Unit 1 (ch. 1-6)

And one called to another and said: "Holy, holy, holy is the LORD of hosts; the whole earth is full of his glory!" Isaiah 6:3, ESV

Why this one: the vision that governs the whole book.

3. Isaiah 7:9b · Unit 2 (ch. 7-12)

"If you are not firm in faith, you will not be firm at all." Isaiah 7:9b, ESV

Why this one: the book's demand in one line; stand by faith or not at all.

4. Isaiah 9:6 · Unit 2 (ch. 7-12)

"For to us a child is born, to us a son is given; and the government shall be upon his shoulder, and his name shall be called Wonderful Counselor, Mighty God, Everlasting Father, Prince of Peace." Isaiah 9:6, ESV

Why this one: the child who is Mighty God; the incarnation foretold.

5. Isaiah 11:9 · Unit 2 (ch. 7-12)

"They shall not hurt or destroy in all my holy mountain; for the earth shall be full of the knowledge of the LORD as the waters cover the sea." Isaiah 11:9, ESV

Why this one: the earth full of the knowledge of the LORD.

6. Isaiah 25:8 · Unit 4 (ch. 24-27)

"He will swallow up death forever; and the Lord GOD will wipe away tears from all faces, and the reproach of his people he will take away from all the earth, for the LORD has spoken." Isaiah 25:8, ESV

Why this one: death swallowed up forever; Paul's resurrection text (1 Cor 15:54).

7. Isaiah 26:3 · Unit 4 (ch. 24-27)

"You keep him in perfect peace whose mind is stayed on you, because he trusts in you." Isaiah 26:3, ESV

Why this one: perfect peace for the mind stayed on Yahweh.

8. Isaiah 30:15 · Unit 5 (ch. 28-35)

"For thus said the Lord GOD, the Holy One of Israel, 'In returning and rest you shall be saved; in quietness and in trust shall be your strength.' But you were unwilling." Isaiah 30:15, ESV

Why this one: in returning and rest you shall be saved; the refused invitation.

9. Isaiah 40:8 · Unit 7 (ch. 40-48)

"The grass withers, the flower fades, but the word of our God will stand forever." Isaiah 40:8, ESV

Why this one: the word of our God stands forever.

10. Isaiah 40:31 · Unit 7 (ch. 40-48)

"but they who wait for the LORD shall renew their strength; they shall mount up with wings like eagles; they shall run and not be weary; they shall walk and not faint." Isaiah 40:31, ESV

Why this one: strength for the waiting; wings like eagles.

11. Isaiah 46:9-10 · Unit 7 (ch. 40-48)

"remember the former things of old; for I am God, and there is no other; I am God, and there is none like me, declaring the end from the beginning and from ancient times things not yet done, saying, 'My counsel shall stand, and I will accomplish all my purpose'" Isaiah 46:9-10, ESV

Why this one: God declares the end from the beginning; the anchor of sovereignty.

12. Isaiah 53:5-6 · Unit 8 (ch. 49-55)

"But he was pierced for our transgressions; he was crushed for our iniquities; upon him was the chastisement that brought us peace, and with his wounds we are healed. All we like sheep have gone astray; we have turned—every one—to his own way; and the LORD has laid on him the iniquity of us all." Isaiah 53:5-6, ESV

Why this one: penal substitution in Isaiah's own words; the heart of the book.

13. Isaiah 55:8-9 · Unit 8 (ch. 49-55)

"For my thoughts are not your thoughts, neither are your ways my ways, declares the LORD. For as the heavens are higher than the earth, so are my ways higher than your ways and my thoughts than your thoughts." Isaiah 55:8-9, ESV

Why this one: his thoughts above our thoughts; grace beyond calculation.

14. Isaiah 61:1 · Unit 9 (ch. 56-66)

"The Spirit of the Lord GOD is upon me, because the LORD has anointed me to bring good news to the poor; he has sent me to bind up the brokenhearted, to proclaim liberty to the captives, and the opening of the prison to those who are bound;" Isaiah 61:1, ESV

Why this one: the verse Jesus read in Nazareth; "Today this is fulfilled" (Luke 4:21).

15. Isaiah 66:2 · Unit 9 (ch. 56-66)

"All these things my hand has made, and so all these things came to be, declares the LORD. But this is the one to whom I will look: he who is humble and contrite in spirit and trembles at my word." Isaiah 66:2, ESV

Why this one: the person God looks to; the book's closing posture.

How to memorize (and keep it)

  • First day: read the verse aloud 10 times, then say it from memory 5 times.
  • Spacing: review tomorrow, then at 3 days, 1 week, 2 weeks, 1 month. Lessons will prompt these reviews.
  • Retrieve, don't reread: always attempt from memory first; only then check the ESV. Effortful recall is what builds retention.
  • Say the reference before and after the verse so the address sticks with the words.
