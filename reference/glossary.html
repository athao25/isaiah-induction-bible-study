Glossary

Every technical term used in the lessons, defined once, used consistently. Grows as the course grows.

Study method

TermDefinition
Inductive studyDrawing conclusions out of the text by observation, rather than bringing conclusions to it. Three ordered steps: observation, interpretation, application.
ExpositionExplaining a passage's God-intended meaning in its context and pressing it on the hearer. "Expositional" study works through a book verse by verse rather than hopping between topics.
Grammatical-historical methodInterpreting a text by its grammar (what the words and sentences say) and its history (what the original author and audience meant and understood).
ContextThe material surrounding a passage: the paragraph, the section, the book, and the whole Bible. The first question of interpretation.
Cross-referenceAnother passage of Scripture that quotes, echoes, or explains the one you are studying. Scripture interprets Scripture.

Theology

TermDefinition
Reformed theologyThe doctrinal stream of the Protestant Reformation: Scripture alone as final authority, salvation by grace alone through faith alone in Christ alone, and God's absolute sovereignty in creation and salvation.
CovenantA formal bond God establishes with people, with promises and obligations. Isaiah prosecutes Judah for breaking the Mosaic covenant (Deuteronomy) and promises the new covenant realities of forgiveness and a transformed people.
HolinessGod's utter set-apartness: both his moral purity and his transcendent otherness. Isaiah's controlling vision of God ("Holy, holy, holy", Isa 6:3).
RemnantThe believing minority God preserves through judgment, out of which he rebuilds his people (Isa 6:13, 10:20-22).
MessiahHebrew "anointed one" (Greek: Christ). The promised Davidic king. In Isaiah he appears as the child-king (7, 9, 11), the Servant (42-53), and the anointed conqueror (59-63).
Servant SongsFour poems about the Servant of Yahweh: Isa 42:1-9, 49:1-13, 50:4-11, 52:13-53:12. Fulfilled in Jesus (Acts 8:32-35).
ZionThe temple mount in Jerusalem, and by extension God's dwelling with his people. Isaiah moves from Zion judged (1:21) to Zion glorified (2:2-4, ch. 60-66).
OracleA weighty prophetic message, often introduced "the oracle concerning..." (Isa 13:1). Chapters 13-23 are oracles against the nations.
EschatologyThe doctrine of last things: how God brings history to its goal. The three Reformed positions on the millennium are premillennial, amillennial, and postmillennial (see the book map card).
PostmillennialismThe view that Christ returns after ("post") an age in which the gospel progressively triumphs among the nations; Isaiah's golden-age pictures increasingly characterize this age. Our course's home lens (Durbin).
TypologyGod-designed patterns where earlier persons, events, or institutions (types) foreshadow and are fulfilled by later, greater realities (antitypes), supremely Christ.
