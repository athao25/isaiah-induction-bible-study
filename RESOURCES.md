# Isaiah Study Resources

## Knowledge

- [ESV Bible online (ESV.org)](https://www.esv.org/Isaiah+1/)
  Primary study text with free interlinear, cross-references, and reading plans. Use for: every daily reading.
- [Grace to You: John MacArthur sermon archive on Isaiah](https://www.gty.org/library/resources/sermons-library/scripture/23/Isaiah)
  Full free audio/transcripts of MacArthur's Isaiah preaching, including the extended Isaiah 53 series ("The Gospel According to God"). Use for: expositional model per passage, especially chapters 6, 9, 40, 53.
- [MacArthur Study Bible (owned, in Olive Tree)](https://www.olivetree.com/)
  Verse notes, book introduction, and outline for Isaiah. Use for: quick context checks after your own observation step, never before it.
- MacArthur Study Bible Isaiah notes, local copy: `reference/macarthur-notes.html`
  The user's personal copy of their owned MSB Isaiah notes, formatted with per-chapter anchors and a lens-caveat header. Private use only; never publish or share this file. Use for: linking a specific chapter's note from a lesson (e.g., `macarthur-notes.html#ch53`).
- [Apologia Church sermon archive (Jeff Durbin)](https://apologiachurch.com/sermons/)
  Durbin's preaching; Reformed Baptist, postmillennial. Use for: kingdom/optimistic-eschatology handling of Isaiah 2, 11, 65-66.
- [Alpha and Omega Ministries / The Dividing Line (James White)](https://www.aomin.org/)
  White's exegetical work, especially Isaiah 53 and deity-of-Christ passages (Isa 6 with John 12:41; Isa 44-45 monotheism). Use for: careful text-driven argument at christological passages.
- [Calvin's Commentary on Isaiah (CCEL, free)](https://www.ccel.org/ccel/calvin/calcom13.html)
  Classic Reformed exposition, 4 volumes, free full text. Use for: the historic Reformed reading of any passage.
- [J. A. Motyer, _The Prophecy of Isaiah_ (IVP)](https://www.ivpress.com/the-prophecy-of-isaiah)
  The standard conservative one-volume commentary; strongest on Isaiah's literary structure (his King / Servant / Conqueror framework shapes our book map). Use for: structure and flow questions. Not owned; optional purchase.
- [E. J. Young, _The Book of Isaiah_ (3 vols)](https://www.eerdmans.com/)
  Older conservative Reformed technical commentary; the classic defense of single authorship. Use for: authorship/unity questions.
- [Blue Letter Bible](https://www.blueletterbible.org/esv/isa/1/1/)
  Free Strong's-linked word study tools on the ESV. Use for: English-based Hebrew word studies (e.g., qadosh, hesed, mishpat).

## Wisdom (Communities)

- Local church (user is a member)
  Primary community. Lessons end with occasional "discuss with your pastor or group" prompts tied to the passage.
- [r/Reformed](https://www.reddit.com/r/Reformed/)
  Well-moderated Reformed subreddit. Use for: questions your local church can't get to, resource recommendations.

- Durbin has no single verse-by-verse Isaiah series; his Isaiah material is scattered across sermons. Pull specific sermons per passage as we reach them.
